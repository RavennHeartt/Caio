Module 03: The Timeline

Present, Past & Future

Trilha Personalizada: Caio Gomes

Instrutor: Matheus Machado

1. The Foundation: Present & Past Review

Let's review what we already know. In business, clarity is everything. O Simple Present is our tool for routines, habits, and absolute facts. Por outro lado, usamos o Simple Past para ações que já morreram, capítulos encerrados.

• Present (Routine): I always check the emails in the morning. (Eu sempre checo...)

• Past (Finished): I checked the emails yesterday. (Eu chequei...)

• Present (Fact): The server is fast. (O servidor é...)

• Past (Finished): The server was down last night. (O servidor estava...)

Mental trigger: If you mention "yesterday", "last week", or "in 2025", the verb MUST be in the past.

2. The Gerund (-ING): Action in Progress

When you are in the middle of a task, you use the Present Continuous. O sufixo -ING é o exato equivalente ao nosso -ANDO, -ENDO, -INDO. To make it work, it always needs the verb To Be as an assistant.

• I am working on the new project right now. (Eu estou trabalhando...)

• They are developing a new software. (Eles estão desenvolvendo...)

• The CEO is waiting in the boardroom. (O CEO está esperando...)

In emails, it is very common to see the -ING after prepositions: "Thank you for sending the report." (Obrigado por enviar...).

3. The Future: WILL vs. GOING TO

To talk about the future in English, we don't just change the verb ending. We use helper words. E aqui está o segredo: Will e Going To não são a mesma coisa no ambiente corporativo.

WILL (Instant Decisions & Promises)

Usamos o "Will" para decisões tomadas no exato momento da fala, promessas ou quando não temos 100% de certeza.

(The phone rings) "I will answer it!" (Eu vou atender / atendo! - Decisão rápida).

"Don't worry, I will send the files by 5 PM." (Promessa de entrega).

GOING TO (Plans & Evidence)

Usamos "Going to" (sempre com o verbo To Be antes) para planos estruturados, coisas já decididas no passado para acontecer no futuro, ou quando há evidências claras.

"We are going to launch the campaign in December." (O projeto já está aprovado, é um plano).

"Look at those numbers. We are going to lose money." (Evidência clara nos dados).

4. Reading Challenge: The Status Update

Identify the timeline. Notice how the manager moves smoothly from the past, to the present, and into the future plans.

"Good morning, team. Yesterday, we finished the primary tests on the database. The results were outstanding. Right now, the engineering team is deploying the secondary servers. They are working hard to maintain stability. Because of this success, we are going to present the final product to the investors next Monday. I will send the presentation draft to everyone this afternoon so you can review it. Thank you for your dedication."

5. Exhaustive Business Roleplays

Speak out loud. Pay attention to the shift in timelines (Past to Present to Future). This is real-world fluency testing.

Simulation 1: The Urgent Fix (Will vs Past)
Matheus "Caio, we have a major problem. The payment gateway crashed."
Caio "Crashed? But it worked perfectly yesterday during the tests."
Matheus "I know. The clients are complaining. Are you fixing it?"
Caio "Yes, I am looking at the code right now. I found a bug in the API."
Matheus "How long is it going to take?"
Caio "Give me ten minutes. I will reboot the system and apply the patch."
Matheus "Okay. I will inform the customer service team."
Caio "Done. It is running again. We are safe."
Simulation 2: Quarter Planning (Going To)
Caio "Matheus, did you see the email from the CEO?"
Matheus "Yes, I saw it this morning. We are going to expand to Europe."
Caio "That is huge. When are we going to start the hiring process?"
Matheus "HR is preparing the budget right now. They are going to open fifty new positions next month."
Caio "Wow. Are we going to translate the entire platform?"
Matheus "Exactly. In fact, I will need your help with the front-end layout."
Caio "No problem. I will schedule a meeting with the UI designers tomorrow."
Matheus "Great. This expansion is going to change everything."
Simulation 3: Project Status Report (Present Continuous)
Matheus "Caio, can you give me a status update on the new app?"
Caio "Sure. Right now, the back-end team is integrating the database."
Matheus "Are they facing any issues?"
Caio "A few minor bugs, but they are solving them quickly."
Matheus "What about the marketing campaign?"
Caio "They are waiting for the final logo design. Sarah is drawing it today."
Matheus "Excellent. I will tell the board that we are on schedule."
Caio "Yes, we are going to deliver the app before the deadline."
Simulation 4: Performance Review (Past vs Future)
Matheus "Have a seat, Caio. Let's talk about your performance this year."
Caio "Thank you, Matheus. I am excited to hear your feedback."
Matheus "In the first semester, you managed the crisis very well. You solved the core issues."
Caio "It was a challenging period, but the team was amazing."
Matheus "Because of your hard work, I am going to promote you to Senior Developer."
Caio "Seriously? Thank you! I will not disappoint you."
Matheus "I know you won't. You are going to lead the new European squad."
Caio "I accept the challenge. I am starting to draft the strategy today."
Simulation 5: Dealing with an Angry Client (Mixed)
Matheus "Caio, the client from New York is on the phone. He is furious."
Caio "Oh no. What happened?"
Matheus "They didn't receive the shipment yesterday. It is delayed."
Caio "Let me check the system. Ah, I see. The logistics company had a strike."
Matheus "What are we going to do? We are going to lose the contract."
Caio "Don't panic. I will call the secondary supplier right now."
Matheus "Are they delivering today?"
Caio "I will pay for premium air shipping. We are going to fix this. Tell the client I am handling it."

6. Executive Dictionary

To Crash: Falhar / Cair (sistema)
Draft: Rascunho
Boardroom: Sala de reuniões
Shipment: Remessa / Envio
Deadline: Prazo final
Outstanding: Excepcional
To Expand: Expandir
Strike: Greve
To Schedule: Agendar
On schedule: No prazo
